When it comes to describing what an RWB Porsche is, the simple answer is that it's a customized, widebody Porsche 911 built by noted Japanese tuner Akira Nakai. But the real answer is far more complicated. These extensively modified 911s, with exaggerated fender flares, gigantic wings, deep-dish wheels, and exposed rivets, make RWB Porsches extremely polarizing and controversial vehicles—especially among the diehard Porsche faithful. RWB-modified cars are viewed as either rolling works of art created by a master car tuner, or blasphemous, caricatured monstrosities created by a rogue car-toon-er. But in a world starved for its next trending IG post, the one thing both sides can agree on is that RWB Porsches will always provoke a reaction.

RWB

Founded in 1997, RWB stands for Rauh-Welt Begriff. In German, rauh-welt roughly translates to "rough world," though the last word (depending on where you choose to look for a translation) can mean either "expression," "style," or "concept." The phrase itself is meant to reflect two separate but related ideas. The first is the admittedly imperfect style in which Nakai imagines his custom machines, sometimes using nothing but his eyes and an air-powered saw to cut a line into a classic Porsche. The second is to mirror the aggressive manner in which Nakai likes to drive his own cars. He believes that sports cars—even one-offs as unique as his creations—are meant to be driven hard and not relegated to being garage prisoners for the entirety of their existence.

History of RWB

Akira Nakai was introduced to Porsches while working at a body shop to help fund his drift club, known as Rauh-Welt, aka "Rough World." The first RWB Porsche started as a beat-up, air-cooled 930-Series 911 that Nakai bought for himself. He cut weight everywhere and anywhere from the car, upgraded the engine to make 360 horsepower, installed a full race suspension, and of course created an incredibly dramatic body kit. The result was a 2,100-pound car he named Stella Artois, after his favorite beer.

Top 10
These Are The 10 Most B**A** Porsches By Rauh Welt Begriff, ranked by hotcars.com
Jezebel
10
Jezebel is Akira's seventh build. Its Guards Red exterior certainly sings seduction. From a 1989 Porsche 911 Turbo to this sweet seductress of a ride, Jezebel's smooth fender design and its engine overhaul showcases the genius of Akira Nakai, who reportedly built Jezebel for his own use. The interior has been upgraded with fresh carpeting, a steering wheel wrapped in leather with deviated stitching, among other modifications. Jezebel has been fitted with 18-inch Work wheels and Pirelli P Zero tires. Its turbocharged engine can deliver 300 horsepower with 270 lb-ft of torque. 'Jezebel' show why RAUH-Welt Begriff is a tuning company every gearhead should know about.
Shinkiro
9
Nakai took a Porsche 930 and turned it into what we know as Shinkiro, featuring that stunning Rauh Welt Begriff bodykit. Built in Russia, Nakai was meticulous with his detailing. Shinkiro participated in the 2018 Modball Rally, alongside a Lamborghini Aventador S, that made its presence felt. But Shinkiro was quite at home taking on the streets with burnouts and creating an awesome display among other sweet rides.
Rotana
8
Rotana is a powerhouse 993 Turbo, capable of 575 horses, with its engine receiving the custom upgrades we expect from RWB, such as a GReddy Turbocharger and custom exhaust system. The body has all the fingerprints of RWB we expect to find, such as their signature carbon fiber roof, rear bumper, aero vented rear fenders and a wing that cannot be overlooked. The purple finish makes the Rotana stand out from the pack.
Baymax
7
The 'Baymax' is a 1997 Porsche 911 Carrera 2, powered by a 3.6-liter flat-six engine, with that Grand Prix White exterior. With many signature RWB details that makes this one sweet ride, such as the super wide street version body with the Kamiwaza superspoiler, street style bumper, among other features. The interior of the 'Baymax' includes the RWB roll bar, Momo Magnus Walker Limited Edition steering wheel and Recaro Alcantara seats.
Natty Dread
6
The RWB 'Natty Dread' is based on a 993 that is powered by a 3.6-liter flat-six, with all the fine touches we expect, such as Rauh Welt widened front fenders, their own signature rear fenders, Rauh Welt side skirts, to name some of the modifications. The interior has been fitted with Recaro bucket seats, not to mention the Premodet exhaust setup and a matte black finish that stamps this as one silky smooth, hot ride.
Ichiban Boshi
5
'Ichiban Boshi' or otherwise referred to as the 'The Highest Star'. From a 1989 Silver Anniversary Carrera, it was soon transformed into the 'Highest Star', powered by a 3.3-liter turbocharged flat-six engine, pumping out a staggering 600 horses. The turbocharger being a custom-fit Honeywell Garrett setup. RWB made every effort to drop any unwanted weight, with the 'Highest Star' weighing in at 2,400 pounds. Check out the body with the carbon hood, carbon roof, signature custom fenders, among other features.
Maverick
4
This RWB Porsche 911 C4 'Maverick' is the perfect mod-job. 'Maverick' has that wide body we just love. RWB collaborated with Misfit Motorsports on the C4 to create something very special. The Sepang Bronze Metallic exterior gives 'Maverick' an air of sinister wickedness, especially when matched with those standout rear fender flares and the massive wing. Fitted with Work Meister S1 3-piece rims, 'Maverick' looks ready to tear up the road.
Spearmint Rhino
3
Based on a 993, transformed into what we now see, a beautiful matte white cabriolet, was the work of the legend himself, Akira Nakai. The name was reportedly inspired by a trip to Las Vegas. 'Spearmint Rhino' was displayed at the 30th Anniversary Tokyo Auto Salon and became one of Nakai's own rides. The wide body we now have come to expect along with the ravishing matte white finish allures the senses. Nakai added his own touches, such as a set of flags at the rear, almost like a personal branding of where he had travelled.
Stella Artois
2
'Stella Artois' is one of those amazing modified Porsche 911s we would love to take for a spin. From a 930 to a hot ride named after Akira Nakai's favorite beer, 'Stella Artois' screams 'drive me!' Nakai made sure to shed any excess pounds from this 911, using carbon fiber for the roof and doors, among other parts, weighing in at 2,100 pounds. The massive rear wing is unmissable and stamps this with an undeniable badass attitude, especially when its 3.8-liter engine that can deliver a sweet 355 horses. Fitted with Work Meister S1s and SSR Professors, 'Stella Artois' was the pioneer leading the power-packed chorus of Nakai's custom Porsches.
Rauh Passion
1
The 'Rauh Passion' is one dreamy 993 Carrera 2, powered by a 3.6-liter flat-six. The succulent exterior is not only seductive with its paint job, but also sings with those fenders and the wing that punctuates this 993 as one sweet ride. Whether it be the seductive allure of the 'Rauh Passion' or the unmatched attitude of the 'Maverick', RAUH-Welt Begriff, one of the most legendary Japanese tuners, bring meaning to their custom-builds. A 930 like Shinkiro can not only own the road alongside a Lambo at the Modball Rally, but makes it own statement in the process. Then to think it all started with Nakai's own 'Stella Artois', the 930 that became the badass pioneer of this journey.
